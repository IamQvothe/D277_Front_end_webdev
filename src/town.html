Oroville

Oroville, California, was established during the California Gold Rush when prospectors settled in the area around the Feather River. Originally named Ophir City, it became a major supply town for gold miners. The city was incorporated on January 3, 1906. This dam created Lake Oroville, a crucial water reservoir and popular recreational site. Today, Oroville serves as the county seat of Butte County and is known for its historical significance and natural beauty.
