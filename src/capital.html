A brief history on Sacramento: Sacramento, the capital city of California, has a rich and varied history dating back to its roots in the mid-19th century. Originally inhabited by the Nisenan people, the area saw a significant transformation with the arrival of Spanish explorers in the late 18th century. The city itself was founded in 1848 by John Sutter, a Swiss immigrant who established Sutter’s Fort. The discovery of gold at Sutter’s Mill in nearby Coloma in 1848 sparked the California Gold Rush, leading to a massive influx of fortune seekers. Sacramento quickly grew as a commercial and agricultural hub due to its strategic location at the confluence of the Sacramento and American Rivers. In 1854, it was designated as the state capital. Over the years, Sacramento has evolved from a bustling Gold Rush town to a vibrant metropolis, playing a pivotal role in the state’s political, economic, and cultural development.
