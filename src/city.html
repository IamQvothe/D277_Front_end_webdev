San Francisco

San Franciscoâ€™s history dates back to the early 18th century when Spanish explorers first sailed into San Francisco Bay in 1769. It was formally founded as a Spanish mission and presidio in 1776. The city, originally named Yerba Buena, was a small settlement until the California Gold Rush of 1848-1849, which transformed it into a bustling metropolis as prospectors and entrepreneurs flooded the area. In 1847, the city was renamed San Francisco and became a vital hub for trade and shipping. The 1906 earthquake and subsequent fires devastated the city, leading to a massive rebuilding effort that shaped much of its modern architecture. San Francisco continued to grow throughout the 20th century, becoming known for its cultural diversity, vibrant arts scene, and progressive values. Today, it is renowned for landmarks like the Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, and its historic cable cars
